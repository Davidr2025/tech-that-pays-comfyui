If someone says "downtown Mississauga," they usually mean the area around
**Square One** — the city's civic and commercial core, built up around one
of the largest shopping malls in Canada. It's a different kind of downtown
than what you'd find in an older city: newer, denser than the rest of
Mississauga, and still actively under construction as more residential
towers go up around it.

## The mall itself

Square One is the anchor — a large enclosed shopping centre with the usual
mix of major retailers, a food court, and a movie theatre. It's also the
hub MiWay bus routes converge on, making it the practical starting point for
getting to most other parts of the city by transit.

## City Centre, beyond the mall

The blocks immediately around Square One make up **Mississauga City Centre**:

- **Mississauga Celebration Square** — the outdoor civic plaza across from
  City Hall, host to most of the city's larger public events (concerts,
  festivals, a seasonal skating rink in winter). Check
  [Things to Do](/events/) for what's currently scheduled there.
- **Mississauga City Hall** — the city's distinctive municipal building,
  worth a look even if you're not there for civic business.
- **Central Library** — the main branch of the Mississauga Library System,
  next to Celebration Square.
- A growing cluster of condo towers and mid-rise buildings, with more street-level
  restaurants and cafés opening as the area densifies — browse our
  [restaurant](/directory/restaurants/) and [café & bakery](/directory/cafes/)
  directories for what's currently around.

## Getting there and around

City Centre is Mississauga's best-connected area by transit — it's the
convergence point for most MiWay routes, with the Mississauga Transitway
bus rapid transit corridor running nearby. It's also one of the more
walkable pockets of the city once you're there, with the mall, Celebration
Square, City Hall, and the library all within a few minutes of each other on
foot. See our [transit guide](/guides/getting-around-mississauga-transit/)
for the wider picture on getting to and from the area.

## Good to know

- Parking at Square One is plentiful but can fill up during peak shopping
  periods and major events at Celebration Square.
- The area keeps changing fast — new towers, new street-level retail — so
  it's worth checking back if it's been a while since your last visit.
