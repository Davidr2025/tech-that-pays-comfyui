Streetsville: A Neighbourhood Guide

Published July 18, 2026 · Mississauga Insider

Streetsville sits in the northwest corner of Mississauga, along the Credit River, and is often nicknamed “the Village in the City.” It’s one of the few parts of Mississauga that still looks and feels like the independent town it once was, rather than a neighbourhood built around a suburban plaza.

A short history

Streetsville dates back to 1819, when Timothy Street financed the survey of the area and built the district’s first flour mill on the Credit River. A string of mill sites along the river made the village a local economic hub through the 1800s. It was incorporated as its own village in 1858, became a town in 1962, and was absorbed into the newly formed city of Mississauga in 1974 as part of regional government reorganization. Queen Street S., the main road through the old town centre, still carries its original name and is lined with heritage homes from the 1800s that today house independent shops, cafés, and restaurants.

Getting there

Streetsville GO Station, on GO Transit’s Milton line, sits a short walk from the Queen Street core. It’s a useful option for commuters, though the Milton line runs primarily on weekday rush-hour schedules rather than all-day or weekend service, unlike the Lakeshore line through Port Credit. MiWay buses also connect Streetsville to the rest of the city. See our transit guide for the fuller picture of getting around without a car.

What’s there

  • Queen Street S. The historic main strip, walkable end to end, with independent restaurants, cafés, and shops occupying converted heritage buildings. Browse our restaurant and café & bakery directories for what’s currently open nearby.
  • Streetsville Memorial Park. A large park along the Credit River that doubles as the site of the neighbourhood’s biggest annual event (below), with river access and green space the rest of the year.
  • The Culham Trail. A shaded multi-use trail following the Credit River through Streetsville, connecting the village core to nearby parks and residential streets. Free, flat, and one of the more pleasant walks in this part of the city; see our Things to Do page for more free, evergreen options like it.

The Bread & Honey Festival

Streetsville’s signature event is the Bread & Honey Festival, running since 1973 and typically held the first weekend of June at Streetsville Memorial Park. The name comes directly from the village’s roots: Timothy Street’s flour mill and the area’s beekeeping tradition, with fresh bread and honey still handed out at the festival today. It draws crowds well beyond the neighbourhood itself, so expect Queen Street and the surrounding streets to be busy. Check our Things to Do page for this and other events as they’re listed.

Good to know

  • Street parking along Queen Street is limited, especially on weekends and during the festival. Municipal lots just off the main strip are generally a more reliable option.
  • Streetsville is separate from, and often confused with, its neighbouring communities like Meadowvale, so double-check an address before assuming it’s within walking distance of the village core.
