Port Credit: A Neighbourhood Guide

Published July 14, 2026 · Mississauga Insider

Port Credit is the lakefront village at the mouth of the Credit River, on Mississauga’s southern edge. It’s one of the oldest settled parts of the city, and still the part that feels least like a suburb.

Getting oriented

The neighbourhood sits where the Credit River meets Lake Ontario. Lakeshore Road East runs through the middle of it as the main commercial strip, with the marina and waterfront parks to the south and residential streets fanning out north toward the Queen Elizabeth Way. Port Credit GO Station puts it roughly a 20-minute train ride from Toronto’s Union Station, which is a big part of why it draws both day-trippers and people looking to live car-light.

What’s there

  • The waterfront trail. Port Credit is a stop on the Waterfront Trail that runs along Lake Ontario, good for a walk or bike ride with lake views most of the way, connecting toward Marie Curtis Park to the east and Jack Darling Memorial Park to the west.
  • The marina. One of the larger marinas on this stretch of the lake, with boat charters and views worth the walk even if you’re not on the water yourself.
  • Lakeshore Road’s restaurant strip. This is the densest concentration of restaurants, cafés, and patios in Mississauga outside of the Square One area. Browse our restaurant directory and café & bakery directory filtered to what’s currently open and highly rated.
  • Jack Darling Memorial Park. A large waterfront park with a dog beach, splash pad, and some of the best sunset views in the city.

Big changes coming

Port Credit is also the southern terminus of the Hazel McCallion Line (formerly called the Hurontario LRT), a light rail line under construction along Hurontario Street that will eventually connect Port Credit up through downtown Mississauga to Brampton. As of mid-2026 it’s still under construction with an expected completion around 2028, so for now getting to and around Port Credit means GO Transit, MiWay buses, driving, or (within the neighbourhood itself) walking. See our transit guide for the current options.

There’s also a large mixed-use redevelopment underway on the former power plant lands along the waterfront, gradually reshaping the eastern edge of the neighbourhood. Worth watching if you’re curious how Port Credit’s waterfront will look over the next several years.

Good to know

  • Street parking on Lakeshore Road fills up fast on summer weekends. The municipal lots just north of the strip are usually a safer bet.
  • The area gets noticeably busier during Southside Shuffle (a long-running blues and jazz festival) and other summer waterfront events. Check our Things to Do page for what’s on.
